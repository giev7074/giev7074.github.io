About Me

My name is Giovanni Evans. I am currently in my third year at the University of Colorado at Boulder pursuing a Bachelor’s Degree in computer science with a minor in data science. I love anything technology or data related, specifically topics that combine the two or incorporate sports. I am originally from Los Gatos, California. In the coming months, I will be working as a Data Science and Analytics Intern for Schneider Electric. This is my first internship so I am eager to test my skills in the professional world of technology. Additionally, I will be studying abroad in Madrid, Spain in the Fall 2023 semester. In my free time I love to utilize the skills I have learned in data science and computer science and apply them to my areas of interest such as sports. For this reason I joined the ‘CU Sports Analytics’ and ‘Data Bufs’ clubs at CU Boulder. Also, I love to be active and utilize the beautiful weather of Boulder. Whether it is going on a hike, skiing, playing soccer, or lifting weights, I try to stay as active as I can!

I put this digital portfolio together in order to make it easier for anyone who is interested to learn about me and what I have been working on in school and in my free time. In this portfolio, you will find my Resume, interests and extracurriculars, projects, and social media. I hope that after browsing this portfolio that you have a better understanding of who I am and what I do!

Extracurriculars

CU Sports Analytics Club

In this club, we analyze certain CU sports statistics and manipulate them to make interesting models. For example, last semester we gathered player statistics on the CU Women’s volleyball team and determined who was best at certain things such as serving, setting, etc. Additionally, in this club we get to listen to guest speakers who work in the field in sports data related areas.

Phi Gamma Delta

I am a member of the Phi Gamma Delta fraternity, commonly known as FIJI. I do many things as a member of this fraternity, but the two most noticeable are service and philanthropy. Since I have been in this fraternity I have done many service events including community clean ups, cooking meals for the less fortunate, and helping with food and supplies distribution to those in the area affected by the fires in 2022. Additionally, I have taken part in various philanthropy events that have raised money for the fight against women’s breast cancer and me’s health.

Data Buffs Club

In this club, we learn new data science topics and ways to use the new tools that we learn. I recently joined this club, but in the three meetings I have gone to, I have learned how to use Power BI, Tableau, and new functions/graphics that I can now use in R. Additionally, we get the privilege of getting professionals in the field of Data Science to come in and explain how the topics we are learning apply to the real world.

Projects

Machine Learning Poker Model

Languages Used: Python

This project uses various machine learning tools such as K Nearest Neighbors, Regression, and Neural Networks to try and emulate the moves that a successful poker player would make at a given part of the game.

Python is the primary language for this project due to its ease of use and shortcuts for cleaning and manipulating data.

I am currently in the process of working on this project with two other students for the course Machine Learning.

BuffList

Languages Used: C++, NodeJS, HTML, CSS, Javascript, SQL

Interactive website for CU students that lets them buy and sell goods and trades, similar to Craig’s List.

SQL used for the back end, C++, Javascript, HTML, and CSS used for front end, NodeJS used for integration.

I developed this project in a group for the course Software Development and Tools.

ConnectFour

Languages Used: Python

Created an artificial intelligence based ConnectFour player that utilized Alpha Beta Pruning and heuristics.

Python was the primary language used for this project due to its dictionaries feature and flexibility.

I developed this project by myself as the final assignment for the course Artificial Intelligence.

NBA Statistics

Languages Used: R

This project uses the NBA dataset for the 2020, 2021, and 2022 season containing various team stats such as win percentage, assists, points, rebounds, etc. It will use regression to determine which of the stats is best at predicting win percentage alone, and then what combination of stats is able to best predict win percentage.

R is the primary language used as this project will be made in Rstudio due to its knitting capabilities.

I worked on this project with another student for the course Applied Regression.

MiniGit

Languages Used: C++

Made a smaller scale version of Github using data structures such as linked lists and binary trees.

C++ was the primary language used for this project due its object oriented nature.

I developed this project by myself as the final assignment for the course Data Structures.
